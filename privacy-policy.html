1. Who this policy covers

This Privacy Policy applies to the Kaset mobile application ("Kaset", "the app", "we", "us", or "our"), a personal media tracker for movies, TV shows, and anime, published for iOS and Android. It describes what information the app does and does not process, how that information is handled, and the choices and rights available to you. By using Kaset, you acknowledge the practices described here. If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use the app. If you have questions, contact us at radwan.albahrani@gmail.com.

2. Information we do not collect

Kaset is designed to work without knowing who you are. Specifically, the app does not:

  • require you to create an account, sign in, or provide an email address, name, phone number, or password;
  • collect or store your real-world identity, contacts, photos, calendar, precise or coarse location, or health data;
  • use advertising identifiers (such as Apple's IDFA or the Android Advertising ID) or ask for App Tracking Transparency permission;
  • include third-party analytics, advertising, marketing, or social-media tracking software development kits (SDKs);
  • maintain servers that receive, store, or process a copy of your library or personal data.

Because the app has no account system and no back-end database of users, we hold no personal profile of you and have no ability to look up, export, or delete your data on your behalf — it simply never leaves your device except as described in Sections 4 and 7.

3. Information stored on your device

Everything you create or track in Kaset is stored locally on your device in a private application database (using on-device SQLite storage) and app preferences. This information stays on your device and is under your control. It includes:

CategoryExamplesWhere it is stored
Library & trackingMovies, shows, and anime you add; watch status and progress; watch history and datesOn your device only
Personal notes & ratingsRatings, reviews, notes, tagsOn your device only
OrganizationLists and collections you createOn your device only
Cached metadataTitles, artwork, descriptions, and details previously fetched for faster reuse and offline viewingOn your device only
App preferencesChosen language and interface settingsOn your device only

We do not receive any of this information. It is included in your device's own backups only if you have enabled operating-system backups (for example, iCloud Backup or Google backup); those backups are governed by Apple's or Google's privacy policies, not ours.

4. How Kaset uses the internet

To show details, artwork, and search results for movies, TV shows, and anime, Kaset sends requests to a public media metadata service:

  • The Movie Database (TMDB) — privacy policy

These requests contain only what is needed to find and display a title — for example, the search text you type, a title's identifier, or the language you have selected so results come back in that language. They do not include your identity, your library, your notes, or any personal profile, because the app has none to send. As with any internet request, TMDB necessarily receives standard technical information (such as your device's IP address and network request headers) in order to route and answer the request; this is inherent to how the internet works and is not used by us to identify or track you. All such requests are made over encrypted HTTPS connections. We do not control TMDB's independent data practices; please review their policy linked above.

Kaset makes no other network calls to send your personal data anywhere. If you send us a support email using the in-app "Contact" option (see Section 17), your email — including any diagnostic details you choose to include — is delivered through your own email app to us, so that we can help you.

5. Importing your data

Kaset can import your existing watch history from other services such as TV Time and Sofa. You start an import by selecting an export file that you already have on your device. The file you choose is read and processed entirely on your device. Its contents are not uploaded to us or to any third party. The imported records are stored locally alongside the rest of your library and are treated exactly like data you entered yourself. Kaset only accesses the specific file you select for an import; it does not scan or read other files on your device.

6. Device permissions

Kaset requests only the minimum access it needs, and only at the moment it is needed:

  • File / document access — used solely so you can pick an import file (Section 5). The system file picker lets you choose one file; Kaset receives only that file.
  • Network access — used to fetch title metadata from TMDB (Section 4) and to download app updates (Section 7).

Kaset does not request access to your location, camera, microphone, contacts, photos, calendar, reminders, or health data.

7. App updates

Kaset uses over-the-air updates (delivered through the Expo / EAS Update service) so we can ship fixes and improvements without always requiring a full app-store release. When the app checks for and downloads an update, standard technical request information (such as your device's IP address, platform, and the app's current version and update channel) is handled by the update infrastructure so it can serve the correct files. These checks are used only to deliver software updates and are not used to build a profile of you or to track your activity. You can review Expo's practices in the Expo privacy policy.

8. Analytics, advertising, and tracking

Kaset contains no analytics SDKs, no advertising, no third-party trackers, and no crash- or usage-reporting that transmits your data off the device. We do not build advertising or behavioral profiles, we do not use cookies or similar cross-app identifiers, and we do not track you across other apps or websites. Any usage statistics you see inside the app (for example, viewing counts or "insights") are computed on-device from your own local data and are never sent anywhere.

9. Data sharing, disclosure, and sale

We do not sell, rent, trade, or share your personal information with third parties for their own purposes, and we do not "sell" or "share" personal information as those terms are defined under U.S. state privacy laws. Because your data stays on your device, there is nothing for us to sell or disclose. The only third parties that receive any data are the metadata and update services described in Sections 4 and 7, and only the limited technical information inherent to making those requests. We may disclose information only if strictly required to do so by law — but in practice we hold no personal data that could be disclosed.

10. Data retention and deletion

Your data is retained on your device for as long as you keep the app installed and choose to keep the data. You are in full control:

  • You can permanently erase everything you have tracked at any time from Settings → Delete all data inside the app. This removes your library, watch history, notes, ratings, tags, and collections.
  • Uninstalling the app removes its local database and preferences from your device.

Because we hold no copy of your data on any server, these actions are complete and irreversible, and there is nothing further for us to delete on your behalf.

11. Security

Your data is stored within the app's private, sandboxed storage on your device, which is isolated from other apps by the operating system and protected by your device's own security (such as your passcode, Face ID, Touch ID, or Android biometric lock). All network requests the app makes are transmitted over encrypted HTTPS (TLS) connections. No method of electronic storage or transmission is ever completely secure, but keeping your data on-device and avoiding accounts and servers meaningfully reduces the ways it could be exposed.

12. Children's privacy

Kaset is a general-audience app and is not directed at children under 13 (or the equivalent minimum age in your jurisdiction, such as 16 in parts of the European Economic Area). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Because the app requires no account and collects no personal data, no such information is gathered from users of any age. If you believe a child has provided us personal information, please contact us; however, as noted, the app is designed so that no personal information is transmitted to us.

13. Your privacy rights (GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA/CPRA)

Depending on where you live, you may have rights over your personal data, such as the right to access, correct, delete, port, or restrict processing of your data, and the right not to be discriminated against for exercising these rights. Kaset is built to honor these principles by design:

  • Access & portability: your data lives on your device, where you can view it directly at any time.
  • Correction: you can edit or update any entry within the app.
  • Deletion ("right to be forgotten"): you can erase all data from within the app or by uninstalling it (Section 10).
  • Objection / restriction: the app performs no profiling, automated decision-making, or targeted advertising to object to.

For residents of the European Economic Area and the United Kingdom: to the limited extent the technical information described in Sections 4 and 7 constitutes personal data, the lawful basis for processing it is our legitimate interest in providing the core app functionality (showing title information and delivering updates) that you have requested. For California residents under the CCPA/CPRA: we do not sell or share personal information, and we do not use sensitive personal information for purposes requiring an opt-out. Since we do not maintain user data on our servers, most requests can be fully satisfied by you directly within the app; if you still have questions or wish to exercise a right, contact us using Section 17.

14. International users and data transfers

Kaset can be used worldwide. Your personal data stays on your device in your country. The limited technical requests to the metadata and update services (Sections 4 and 7) may be processed on servers located in other countries, including the United States, operated by those independent providers under their own privacy policies. By using the app, you understand that these standard network requests may be handled internationally.
